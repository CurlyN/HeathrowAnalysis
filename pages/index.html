Breathe Heathrow

Maintaining a network of air quality sensors across the Heathrow area, Breathe Heathrow is a project managed by OpenSensors.io, funded by the Open Data Institute as part of it's Summer Showcase. The project seeks to experiment with and understand new data-driven methodology for air quality measurement.

The graphs and mapping on this site are a representation of the air quality data measured by the sensor network. All of our sensors publish Open Data to the OpenSensors.io platform, where it is freely accessible, shareable and usable by all through an Open Data licence.

The Breathe Heathrow project uses Air Quality Egg V2 air quality sensors, modified to include noise measurement. You can find out more about the Air Quality Egg project and community at airqualityegg.com.

The map shows the live state of sensors in the residential areas surrounding Heathrow Airport and a visualisation of the air quality levels in the area.

To derive this map from the air quality sensor data, we use a process called interpolation. This is a method for finding the value of intermediate values having a discrete set of known values in computational mathematics. The result is a graphical representation consisting of isolines and isobands based on the sensor data.
